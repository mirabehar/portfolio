My Work

Air Pollution Injustice: Mapping PM 2.5 Levels Across Puget Sound

In this capstone GIS project, I analyzed concentrations of PM 2.5 air pollution across the Puget Sound region in 2022. The outcome of my data analysis demonstrates the correlation between severity of exposure and race, ethnicity, and income. ArcGIS is one of my favorite tools to work with, allowing me to work at the intersection of my interests in coding, data analysis, environmental science, and design.

Spoiler Detection Binary Classifier

In this research project, a classmate and I designed an implemented a binary classifier (NLP model) for spoiler detection in Goodreads book reviews using text and metadata features. Our goal was to emulate the performance of a hierarchical attention network, based on experimentation with the NLTK Naive Bayes, Sklearn Multinomial Naive Bayes, and Linear SVC (Support Vector Classification) models. This project was completed over the course of a month, from the initial research and proposal to final presentation. We didn't achieve the level of accuracy we hoped during this time, but I would love to redesign this model in the future with applications across other review-based social platforms, such as Letterboxd.
